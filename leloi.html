The Distorted Legend of Lê Lợi

print, research, exhibition
project overview
The Distorted Legend of Lê Lợi is a personal take on the traditional Vietnamese Đông Hồ woodblock prints, utilizing elements of the original artwork along with my interest in Japanese-inspired character art. During my research, there was little information about Vietnamese mythology which was easily accessible to myself. In addition, much of the information I did find would be contradictory due to the walls of censorship, translation, and time. The wide berth of interwoven history and fable was captivating but I wished that this knowledge were accessible in the same way that other cultures are through fantasy media like Percy Jackson or the various Journey to the West adaptations. This print series hopes to introduce others to a sample of Vietnamese mythology to raise awareness of its existence.
